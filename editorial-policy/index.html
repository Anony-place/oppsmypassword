Editorial Policy

Effective date: February 28, 2026

Editorial Purpose

OopsMyPassword publishes cybersecurity guidance focused on practical, testable user actions. Content is designed to reduce avoidable account and data exposure risk through structured workflows.

We prioritize clarity over jargon and measurable controls over fear-based claims.

Source and Evidence Standards

Primary references should come from standards organizations, official platform documentation, and established security authorities. Secondary commentary is used only when it adds practical context and does not contradict primary guidance.

Each guide must include source references, publication/update dates, and review metadata.

Review Workflow

Drafts are reviewed for technical correctness, implementation realism, and user comprehension under stress conditions.

Major content updates trigger revised review dates and change-log entries to maintain transparency.

Corrections Policy

Confirmed factual errors are corrected promptly. Correction requests should include the page URL, disputed statement, and a reliable source.

Substantive corrections are reflected in page-level update metadata and guide change logs.

Monetization and Ad Placement

Editorial decisions are independent of monetization. We do not create low-value pages for ad inventory expansion.

Ad script placement is intentionally limited to monetizable content routes such as home, guide hub/detail, FAQ, best practices, and selected high-context policy pages.

Legal utility pages and tool execution pages remain ad-free to protect task focus and reduce policy risk.

Some high-intent guides may contain clearly marked affiliate recommendations. These placements use sponsored link attributes and do not alter source requirements, fact-checking standards, or update cadence.

AI and Automation Use

Automation may assist in drafting, formatting, and consistency checks, but final publication requires editorial verification and policy review.

We do not publish unverified generated claims or fabricated references.
